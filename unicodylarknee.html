Unicondylar knee replacement is suitable for patients with:

  • Isolated medial or lateral compartment osteoarthritis
  • Intact anterior cruciate ligament (ACL)
  • Low body mass index (BMI), typically < 30
  • Minimal or no deformity (varus/valgus < 15Â°)
  • Failure of non-operative management (e.g., injections, bracing, physical therapy)

Clinical Decision Criteria:

  • Localized knee pain confined to a single compartment
  • Full or nearly full range of motion
  • No significant inflammatory arthritis (e.g., rheumatoid)
  • Radiographic confirmation of unicompartmental disease

ðŸ”ª Surgical Technique

Minimally Invasive Approach

  • Incision: 3-4 inch medial parapatellar or subvastus
  • Bone preparation: Focused on affected compartment
  • Ligaments: ACL and lateral/medial structures preserved
  • Fixation: Cemented or cementless depending on patient factors

Implant Components

  • Metal femoral component: Cobalt-chrome
  • Tibial component: Metal baseplate with polyethylene insert
  • Options: Mobile vs fixed bearing
  • Material: Similar to total knee implants but smaller

Note: Robotic-assisted UKR helps improve alignment and reduce revision risk in select patients.
