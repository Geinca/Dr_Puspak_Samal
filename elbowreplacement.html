Total Elbow Arthroplasty is considered for patients with:

  • Severe rheumatoid arthritis
  • Post-traumatic arthritis or complex distal humerus fractures
  • Failed previous elbow surgeries
  • Unreconstructable acute fractures in elderly patients
  • Non-union of distal humerus

Clinical Decision Criteria:

  • Persistent elbow pain unresponsive to conservative treatments
  • Joint instability or deformity with functional limitation
  • Radiographic evidence of joint destruction or deformity

ðŸ”ª Surgical Technique

Approaches

  • Posterior approach: Most commonly used
  • Triceps-reflecting or sparing techniques used
  • Ulnar nerve is identified and protected

Implant Components

  • Linked prostheses: Humeral and ulnar components connected with a hinge
  • Unlinked prostheses: Rely on soft tissue for stability
  • Fixation: Typically cemented

Note: Linked implants are more commonly used due to improved stability, especially in rheumatoid or elderly patients.

ðŸ©º Postoperative Recovery

Hospital Stay (1-3 days)

  • Initial immobilization with posterior splint
  • Pain control and early passive movement

First 4 Weeks

  • Progressive physical therapy to regain motion
  • Triceps precautions if reattached
  • Restrictions on lifting and weight-bearing

1-3 Months

  • Increase in activity and strengthening exercises
  • Light ADLs resumed around 6-8 weeks
  • Heavy lifting (over 5-10 lbs) permanently restricted
