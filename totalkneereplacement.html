Total knee replacement is considered for patients with:

  • Severe osteoarthritis (90% of cases)
  • Rheumatoid arthritis unresponsive to medical management
  • Post-traumatic arthritis with joint destruction
  • Advanced osteonecrosis
  • Certain knee tumors requiring reconstruction

Clinical Decision Criteria:

  • Persistent pain unresponsive to 6+ months conservative treatment
  • Significant functional limitation (inability to walk >3 blocks)
  • Radiographic evidence of advanced joint degeneration (Kellgren-Lawrence Grade 3-4)
  • Patient age typically 50-80 years (though increasingly performed in younger patients)

ðŸ”ª Surgical Technique

Standard Approach

  • Incision: 8-12 inch anterior midline
  • Approach: Medial parapatellar (most common)
  • Bone cuts: Femur, tibia, and patella resurfacing
  • Implant fixation: Cemented (most common) vs cementless

Component Options

  • Fixed-bearing: Standard polyethylene insert
  • Mobile-bearing: Rotating platform
  • CR vs PS: Cruciate retaining or posterior stabilized
  • Material: Cobalt-chrome femur, titanium tibia, polyethylene spacer

Note: Computer navigation and robotic-assisted TKA are increasingly used for improved precision.
