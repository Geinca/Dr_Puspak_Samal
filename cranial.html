Cranial therapy, also known as craniosacral therapy, is a gentle, non-invasive treatment that focuses on the craniosacral system, which includes the membranes and cerebrospinal fluid surrounding the brain and spinal cord. This form of therapy is used to relieve tension in the central nervous system and promote a feeling of well-being by eliminating pain and boosting health and immunity.

  • Chronic neck and back pain
  • Stress and tension-related disorders
  • Motor-coordination impairments
  • Infant and childhood disorders
  • Brain and spinal cord injuries
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Fibromyalgia

üëê How It Works

The therapist uses their hands to evaluate the craniosacral system by gently feeling various locations of the body. The soft touch techniques release restrictions in the tissues influencing the craniosacral system, improving the function of the central nervous system. Sessions typically last 45 to 60 minutes and are deeply relaxing.

üí¨ What to Expect

During a session, you will lie fully clothed on a massage table in a quiet setting. The therapist gently touches your head, neck, back, and other areas to assess the ease of motion and rhythm of the cerebrospinal fluid. Patients often experience a sense of calm and balance following a session.

  • Enhances immune system
  • Improves sleep and mental clarity
  • Relieves emotional stress and trauma

Cranial therapy is generally considered safe for most individuals. However, people with recent skull fractures, cerebral aneurysms, or severe bleeding disorders should consult a healthcare provider before receiving treatment. Mild side effects such as temporary soreness or fatigue may occur but typically resolve quickly.
