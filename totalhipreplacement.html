Total Hip Replacement is recommended for patients with:

  • Advanced osteoarthritis of the hip
  • Rheumatoid arthritis or other inflammatory arthropathies
  • Avascular necrosis of the femoral head
  • Post-traumatic arthritis or failed previous surgeries
  • Significant hip pain interfering with daily life and mobility

Clinical Decision Criteria:

  • Radiographic evidence of joint space narrowing, osteophytes
  • Failed non-surgical treatments like NSAIDs, injections, therapy
  • Progressive limitation of motion or quality of life

ðŸ”ª Surgical Technique

Approaches

  • Posterior approach: Most common, good exposure
  • Anterior approach: Muscle-sparing, faster recovery
  • Lateral approach: Alternative for certain anatomies

Implant Components

  • Acetabular cup: Metal shell with polyethylene/ceramic liner
  • Femoral stem: Titanium or cobalt-chrome alloy
  • Head: Ceramic or metal
  • Fixation: Cemented or uncemented based on bone quality

Note: Robotic-assisted THR and navigation systems are increasingly used for enhanced accuracy.
